Nasheed Jafri

Hello! I am a Mathematics PhD student at Indiana University Bloomington, with a doctoral minor in Data Science. I am also concurrently pursuing a Master's in Applied Statistics to complement my mathematical background and interest in data-driven models, Statistical Analysis, and Machine Learning.

Previously, I studied Mathematics at the Indian Institute of Technology, Delhi, where I developed a good foundation in both theoretical and applied aspects of Mathematics.

My PhD advisor is Hari Bercovici. This is us at Hari's 70th birthday conference in Atlanta :)

My research interests lie primarily in Functional Analysis, with a focus on Linear Algebra and Matrix Theory. I have been studying the uniqueness of invariant subspaces of a certain class of nilpotent matrices, particularly in the context of numbers called Littlewood-Richardson coefficients. These numbers are associated with combinatorial objects known as honeycombs or hives. For a layman-friendly glimpse of the structures I work with, here are some interesting pictures: Honeycombs, Young Tableaux.
