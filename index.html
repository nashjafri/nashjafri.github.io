Nasheed Jafri

Hello! I am a Mathematics PhD student at Indiana University Bloomington, with a doctoral minor in Data Science. I am also concurrently pursuing a Master's in Applied Statistics to complement my interests and mathematical background.

Previously, I studied Mathematics at the Indian Institute of Technology, Delhi, and I have a strong foundation in both theoretical and applied aspects of Mathematics.

My PhD advisor is Hari Bercovici. My research interests lie primarily in Functional Analysis, with a focus on Linear Algebra and Matrix Theory. I have been studying the uniqueness of invariant subspaces of a certain class of nilpotent matrices, particularly in the context of combinatorial structures called Littlewood-Richardson coefficients.

Apart from my mathematical research, I enjoy working with data-driven models, Statistical Analysis, and Machine Learning.
